The Source Material

What the Congo Hold-Up Found

In June 2023, the African Investigative Publishing Collective (ACDC) and Platform FACT released the results of a two-year investigation into BGFIBank DRC, a commercial bank operating inside the Democratic Republic of Congo during the presidency of Joseph Kabila. The investigation involved more than 100 journalists across 19 media partners and drew on 3.5 million leaked financial documents.

What they found was a complete merger of state and private financial power. BGFIBank DRC was not merely a bank that had proximity to political power. It was an instrument of extraction. A machine designed to move public money into private accounts controlled by a single family.

The Bank

BGFIBank DRC

Subsidiary of BGFIBank Group, the Gabon-based parent. Operated as the DRC's preferred state bank during the Kabila years. Francis Selemani Mtwale, Kabila's adopted brother, served as CEO.

The Family

The Kabila Network

Joseph Kabila (President, 2001 to 2019), his adopted brother Francis Selemani (bank CEO), and his sister Gloria Mteyu (shareholder). Family members held roles on both sides of every transaction: lender and borrower simultaneously.

The Mechanism

State Money → Private Pockets

DRC state institutions (including state mining company Gécamines and the Central Bank, the BCC) deposited funds into BGFIBank DRC. The bank then issued loans to Kabila-linked companies that were never repaid. $138M+ documented. Believed to be a fraction of total.

Regulatory Capture

The Central Bank Was Compromised

The BCC (Banque Centrale du Congo), the entity responsible for overseeing BGFIBank, deposited its own reserves into BGFIBank DRC, creating a circular conflict of interest. The regulator became a client of the institution it was supposed to regulate.

The Shell Layer

Offshore Architecture

Loans flowed to companies registered in BVI and other offshore jurisdictions, controlled by Kabila-linked entities. Panora Trust, a Kabila family trust, appeared across multiple transactions as a controlling vehicle.

International Consequences

US DOJ Action

BGFIBank Group (the Gabon parent) entered a deferred prosecution agreement with the US Department of Justice in 2023 over money laundering violations. The bank admitted to laundering money on behalf of sanctioned parties and paid over $1.8 billion in penalties and disgorgement.

The American Version

What's Happening at Polymarket and Kalshi

Polymarket is the world's largest prediction market. In the 2024 US election cycle it processed billions of dollars in event contracts. Users betting real money on political outcomes, sporting events, and geopolitical events. Kalshi is a federally regulated exchange doing similar work under CFTC oversight. Together they represent the frontier of a new financial product: commodified uncertainty.

The problem is not the concept. It is the architecture of who owns it, who regulates it, and whether the regulator has been compromised in the same way the BCC was compromised in the DRC. Not through bribery or force, but through the conversion of the regulatory body into a financially aligned stakeholder.

The Equity Stake

Trump Jr. / 1789 Capital → Polymarket

Donald Trump Jr., through his investment firm 1789 Capital, holds a documented equity stake in Polymarket reportedly in the double-digit millions range. The investment was made as Polymarket's political event volume surged during the 2024 election cycle. Trump Jr. profits as the platform's valuation grows. Not from individual bets. From the ecosystem itself.

The Advisory Role

Trump Jr., Paid Advisor at Kalshi

Donald Trump Jr. holds a separate paid strategic advisor role at Kalshi, a CFTC-regulated platform. This means Trump Jr. simultaneously holds financial positions in both of the platforms the CFTC is actively shielding from enforcement action, while the Trump administration controls CFTC appointments.

The Regulatory Shield

CFTC Enforcement Collapse

The CFTC (Commodity Futures Trading Commission) under Trump administration leadership dropped enforcement actions against Polymarket, reduced overall enforcement activity by approximately 80% from prior administration levels, and declined to challenge expanded event contract offerings that critics argued enabled market manipulation.

The Crypto Layer

World Liberty Financial

The Trump family crypto venture. Raised an estimated $500M through token sales. House Democrats called for a Treasury Department probe after tokens were sold to entities with connections to sanctioned parties. The structure mirrors the Kabila shell-company layer: a vehicle for converting political proximity into liquid financial value.

The Anonymous Layer

Wallets Without Names

Bubblemaps forensic analysis identified multiple anonymous wallet clusters exploiting Polymarket's information asymmetry. A 9-wallet operation linked to Iranian IP addresses placed coordinated bets. Individual accounts (Van Dyke, Skoobidoobnj, 0xoracle) demonstrated documented advance knowledge of outcomes. All operated anonymously on-chain.

The Sovereign Fund

US Sovereign Wealth Fund (EO, Feb 2025)

Trump signed an executive order in February 2025 directing the creation of a US Sovereign Wealth Fund. Public money under direct executive control, no established independent oversight mechanism. If implemented, this replicates the most dangerous element of the BGFIBank structure exactly: public funds flowing into an institution controlled by the political family profiting from it.

For the Record

What's Different, and Why That Makes It Worse

Credibility requires accounting for the differences. The parallel is structural but it is not identical. Here is what distinguishes the two cases, and why in each instance the difference cuts against the US version being less concerning rather than more.

  • The DRC is a kleptocracy by design. The US has institutional safeguards. True. But the US safeguards are being systematically dismantled. CFTC enforcement is down 80%. DOJ independence has been challenged through firing and leadership replacement. Treasury declined to probe World Liberty Financial. The institution still exists. The function is being hollowed out. The BCC still existed too. It just stopped doing its job.
  • The family member in the DRC literally ran the bank. Trump Jr. is an outside investor. Also true, and exactly the point. The US version is one layer removed. That layer creates deniability. It makes the conflict harder to litigate and harder to prove. It does not make the conflict smaller. It makes the accountability path longer. In a country with a free press and functioning courts, you design the extraction to survive those systems. That is an upgrade, not a mitigation.
  • The DRC lacked a free press. The US has one. Partially true. Deebs asked 60 Minutes to obscure his identity because he fears physical retaliation from crypto "wrench attacks." The investigative capacity exists. The personal safety of the investigators is not guaranteed. And Hornsby, who documented the DRC case and is now documenting the US parallel, has received no cooperation from US regulatory bodies.
  • The scale is different: $138M in the DRC vs. undisclosed equity in the US. The DRC figure is the documented minimum from one investigation of one bank. BGFIBank Group's DOJ settlement was $1.8B. The US equity stakes are undisclosed. World Liberty Financial raised approximately $500M. Polymarket's valuation at the time of 1789 Capital's investment was in the billions. The US scale is larger. It is simply less documented.
  • Kabila's case involved explicit loans to family members. The US case involves equity appreciation. Yes. This is the most important structural distinction. BGFIBank issued fraudulent loans that were never repaid: a direct, traceable theft. The US version extracts value through platform valuation growth driven by regulatory favoritism. The mechanism is built to be defensible. Which is why Hornsby says: "This is more sophisticated than what I saw in the DRC. It's the same structure wearing a different suit."
Working Thesis

The sophistication of the corruption is a feature, not a bug. You cannot build a kleptocratic machine inside a functioning democracy without making it look defensible. Everything that makes this harder to prosecute in the US than in the DRC is evidence that it was designed by people who know how prosecution works.
