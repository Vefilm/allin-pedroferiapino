The DARPA Precedent

Congress killed this idea in 72 hours. Then built it themselves, 23 years later.

In 2003, DARPA proposed a prediction market to gather intelligence on Middle East instability. The plan included a terrorism market, the logic being that if an insider planned an attack and placed a bet, the price signal would reveal the threat. Congress shut it down in two days. The plan was called "morally repugnant." The person who designed it resigned.

In 2024, Polymarket, registered offshore in Panama, exempt from US regulation, is running markets on military strikes, presidential actions, and geopolitical outcomes. The CFTC, under Michael Zelig, has dropped all enforcement actions. The idea Congress killed in 72 hours is now a $15 billion private business operating at the pleasure of the president's family.
