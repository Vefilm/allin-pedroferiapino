Logline

The Film in Two Sentences

America's $150-billion-a-year sports betting boom has a body count. The companies feeding the bonfire, FanDuel, DraftKings, Kalshi, the NFL itself, are backed by an administration with personal financial stakes in the industry and a Senate that just had to ban its own members from the platforms.

ALL IN is the story of the new American casino, the young men dying inside it, and the people getting rich off the bleeding.

Visual Approach

The Phone Is the Antagonist.

We shoot it like one. Blue screen-glow on faces in dark rooms. Vertical compositions inside the apps, designed to feel like the slot-machine dopamine loop they actually are. Anamorphic, Kodachrome-leaning palette for the world outside the addiction. Verite in the recovery rooms.

The whole film is built around the contrast between the engineered intimacy of the screen and the very real bodies it has been chewing through.

Touchstones: The Social Dilemma's structural urgency. Crime of the Century's institutional rage. All the Beauty and the Bloodshed's restraint.
